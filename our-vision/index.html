OUR VISION

The Vision Behind MalaikAir’s African-Caribbean Connection

African Aviation Advocates (AAA) Founder Adeleke Mark A. sits down with Prince Richard, the visionary Founder and Group CEO of MalaikAir, to discuss his mission of bridging the gap between Africa and the Caribbean through direct air connectivity. With over two decades of experience in aviation consultancy and a deep understanding of the aviation industry, Prince Richard shares the inspiration behind MalaikAir, the challenges faced, and what sets his airline apart from others in the region. This in-depth conversation explores everything from luxury passenger experiences to the airline’s future expansion plans and its commitment to sustainability.

Background and Vision

My name is Prince Richard, and I am the CEO and Chairman of MalaikAir. My background spans Commercial and General Management, Consumer Products, Aviation, and Consultancy.

The vision for MalaikAir has been developing for over two decades, and I began actively working on it about six or seven years ago. I observed a connectivity gap between Africa, the Caribbean, and Latin America, despite their proximity. Several challenges, particularly regulatory and diplomatic issues, needed to be addressed. For example, Nigerian travelers face multiple visa hurdles when traveling to the Bahamas, making the process almost impossible. This highlighted the need to establish better diplomatic relations, encouraging Caribbean nations to offer visa-free or visa-on-arrival options for Africans. We’ve been working on connecting Nigeria to the Caribbean, with eight countries already accessible. We are still working on securing access to the Bahamas and Jamaica, aiming for full connectivity soon.

Progress and Key Milestones

After two decades of effort and seven years of focused work, we’re on the verge of launching by the end of next month. We’re also proud to be the official carrier of the Ipada Carnival, which is part of the Motherland Beacons initiative. This project encourages the African diaspora to return home and will run for the next decade. MalaikAir is also committed to supporting African tourism and enhancing intra-African movement, while working closely with initiatives like the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA).

The Ipada event has been in the background through Motherland Beacons, which has held about 95 weekly meetings. Now it has culminated in the official launch. We are thrilled to have our Global Brand Ambassador, His Excellence President Bola Ahmed Tinubu, backing this initiative, alongside the Nigerian Tourism Ministry and several states, including Lagos and Ekiti. This is all thanks to the founder of Motherland Beacons, Otunba Wanle Akinboboye, who is known for his tourism expertise.

Unique Value Proposition

Our business proposition sets us apart. First, we are very targeted and focused on connecting Africa, particularly West Africa, to the Caribbean and Latin America. Second, we aim to make life easier for our customers by ensuring that their travel is specific and targeted. Third, our customer experience is unrivaled and unparalleled. We offer local dishes, full-service, and cultural initiatives, while embracing diversity. Our staff is shortlisted from 15 countries—12 in Africa and 3 in the Caribbean.
